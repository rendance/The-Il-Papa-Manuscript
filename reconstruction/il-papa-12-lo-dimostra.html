Notes

  • Note that feet in red are explicit in the text.
  • One might normally think the riprese in measures 60 and 61 are first left, then right, but strict alternation of feet would then have the dance ending on the left foot - odd, but not impossible (though it would involve the only instance in the manuscript of a measure of two passi right then left). One could have both going left, which would end the dance on the normal feet, but itself seems a bit odd - again, it might be the only instance in the manuscript of two riprese in a row going in the same direction - or maybe not, as we have interpretted sequential riprese as meza riprese in other places, I believe. Then again, this might simply be two meza riprese here, which would solve all problems - but wouldn't, strictly speaking, be what the text says.
