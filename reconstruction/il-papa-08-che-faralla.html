notes

  • Note that feet in red are explicit in the text.
  • Note that steps in sea green are conjecture (see next note)
  • The section labeled "music" indicates the section of the music in which each step falls
  • Note this is the only dance in the entire manuscript that doesn't end with a et e Finita". Is it unfinished? We don't know. Based on musical evidence, we suspect a final Volta di Lasso.
  • This same musical evidence has highly influenced our reconstruction; our original reconstruction had each figure ending with a reverence, and did not have the odd timing of the A section of the music - but only had 9 bars per section, and assumed even tempo throughout.
  • In measure 8, the foot on which the riverenze is semi-explicit, but not completely explicit - the implication in the following measure is that the right foot on which the lady is doing her riverenza is unusual, thereby implying that the previous one should be left.

  • Something is wrong around measure 18/19/20. At the end of the first section, the manuscript explicitly stated feet in a way that resolved nicely at the beginning of the second section. Here, at the transition from the second to the third figure, it explicitly states feet again - but in a way that not only doesn't resolve neatly, but has the sides and center possibly hitting each other soon, given some interpretations.

    This is the only instance we've seen of mezza ripresa intraverso, so there's very little to go on to determine what it means. We are inclined to interpret it to mean flanking riprese - i.e., travelling a little forwards while going sideways - especially as the author says to do them "straight through the room" - which seems to imply they travel either forwards or backwards.

    It may be that they are still facing opposite directions - hence have to be on opposite feet to avoid coliding during the riprese. But they take hands after the riprese, so these steps must move forward very little - 'in traverso' basically means 'sideways', or at least nearly so (which seems redundant for a ripresa). But this makes one wonder what 'diritto della sala' ('straight through the room') means

    Of course, being on opposite feet after measure 19 propagates through the rest of the dance, including several sections where it is quite odd - so it may simply be a mistake, that he is again trying to specify feet as he did at the end of section 1, and due to a scribal error, writes in the wrong foot.

    Or there may just be an unmentioned fudging of feet somewhere.
