This is the famous "Volta di Lasso".

It is four steps in one measure, very close to a quick side-and-turn-single. The siding is left-then-right, and the turn is over the right shoulder, two steps starting with the left foot.

Notes

  • Note that feet in red are explicit in the text.
  • Why is the Volta di Lasso interpretted as so fast? We have 2 instances of it being performed by one person, while another performs something else (La Traditora, 6:11:19-21 and Che Faralla, 8-10). In la Traditora, the men do a Volta di Lasso, while the lady does 4 passetti. In che Faralla, the men do a Volta di Lasso while the lady does 5 passettini. In both cases, the lady is taking a sinle measure of music, implying the length of this volta.
  • Could the volta di Lasso be LLLR instead of LRLR?
  • Section 3, the contractictory part, could be interpretted as: they stand in a circle, facing outwards, and they each leave, one by one, with three trapassini and a passo. This would implement everything specified with no contradiction. It would also change the length of the figure (which is not, in and of itself, a problem)

  • We need music. Pattern should be:

    C1C2 | AAA C1C2 | AAA C1C2 | AA BC'1 C2

    Each letter represents 2 measures. B could equal A, C'1 could equal C1

    Alternatively, the last figure could be:

    AAA AAA BC'1 C2
