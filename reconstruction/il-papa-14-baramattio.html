Notes

  • Note that feet in red are explicit in the text.
  • The biggest oddity in this dance is, of course, the chorus - something that looks identical to a volta di Lasso. Yet in section 3, we have an explicit volta di Lasso right before it. If it is the same thing, why isn't it labeled as such? It can't be because he just decides not to for this dance, since he does use the label. Perhaps this means there is something specific about the volta di Lasso of which we are unaware? Perhaps, as we have interpretted here, it is slower? Or perhaps this is all over-interpretation, and we shouldn't worry so much!
